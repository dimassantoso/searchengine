Chen upsets Axelsen to claim China crown

Sixth seed Chen Long of China caused an upset in the men’s singles final of the TAHOE China Open when he defeated Denmark’s top seed Viktor Axelsen to win the event in Fuzhou on Sunday.

In a match lasting one hour and 12 minutes, Chen beat Axelsen 21-16, 14-21, 21-13 at the Haixia Olympic Sports Center.

This was the 12th time the two had faced each other in tournaments with Chen leading the head-to-head meetings 10-2.

In the women’s singles final, things went according to the seedings as fifth seed Akane Yamaguchi of Japan cruised past China’s unseeded Gao Fangjie 21-13, 21-15 in just 41 minutes.

The men’s doubles final was dominated by Indonesian top seeds Marcus Fernaldi Gideon and Kevin Sanjaya Sukamuljo who hammered Danish second seeds Mathias Boe and Carsten Mogensen 21-19, 21-11.

The women’s doubles final was a tight affair but China’s second seeds Chen Qingchen and Jia Yifan needed 64 minutes to down Korea Republic eighth seeds Hye Rin Kim and Lee So Hee 21-7, 18-21, 21-14.
